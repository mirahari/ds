Linear Regression¶

$$ REV \hat ENUE = \theta _0 + \theta _1 BUDGET$$
In [32]:
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression
regression = LinearRegression()

# Explanatory Variable(s) or Feature(s)
X = pd.DataFrame(data_clean, columns=['USD_Production_Budget'])
 
# Response Variable or Target
y = pd.DataFrame(data_clean, columns=['USD_Worldwide_Gross']) 

# Find the best-fit line
regression.fit(X, y)
Out[32]:
LinearRegression()
In [33]:
print(f"At $0 budget, a movie will earn ${regression.intercept_[0]:.2f}.")

At $0 budget, a movie will earn $-7572159.92.
In [34]:
print(f"For every extra $1 in the budget, revenue increases by ${regression.coef_[0,0]:.2f}.")

For every extra $1 in the budget, revenue increases by $3.11.
In [35]:
# R-squared (Goodness of Fit)
print(f"This model explains about {regression.score(X, y)*100:.2f}% of the variance in revenue.")

This model explains about 55.70% of the variance in revenue.
